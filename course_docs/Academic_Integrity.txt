# Academic Integrity
Introduction
HKBU aims at developing all aspects of the Whole Person through education. Students are expected to excel academically and become responsible global citizens. The first Graduate Attribute - Citizenship, cultivates students to be responsible citizens with international outlook, ethics and civility. High academic integrity standards are required for all staff and students.

Academic integrity upholds six core fundamental values adapted from the International Center for Academic Integrity (2021):
1. Honesty: Be truthful about borrowed work, ideas and opinions
2. Trust: Build a respectful and fair campus community
3. Fairness: Complete independent work without plagiarizing others' ideas
4. Respect: Value diverse opinions and cite borrowed sources properly
5. Responsibility: Abide by integrity standards and take accountability for personal work
6. Courage: Resist plagiarism and academic cheating temptations

Definition of Academic Misconduct
All actions violating academic integrity, including exam rule breaches, cheating, plagiarism, impersonation, purchasing exam materials, data falsification and dishonest acts for academic credit (National Academic Integrity Network, 2021).

HKBU attaches great importance to academic integrity for students' holistic development and future careers; all students must maintain academic integrity at all times.

Consequences of Academic Dishonesty
Penalties include reduced grades, failing grades, study suspension or permanent academic dismissal.

Students' Core Responsibilities
1. Understand academic integrity rules and avoid dishonest behaviors strictly
2. Submit original independent work and present research findings honestly
3. Cite and reference all external sources correctly
4. Consult instructors for clarification when encountering doubts

Specific Types of Serious Academic Misconduct
1. Collusion: Jointly completing assignments for individual submission; lending work to others; allowing others to copy work
2. Ghost writing: Having others complete partial or full assignments on behalf of oneself
3. Essay and paper mills: Purchasing or downloading ready-made/machine-generated assignments from online platforms
4. Research fraud: Intentionally fabricating, falsifying or misrepresenting research data/methods

Note
Refer to official exam regulations for detailed handling rules and penalties for exam breaches and cheating incidents; the rules also apply to research postgraduate taught courses.
