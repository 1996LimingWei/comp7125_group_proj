Academic Advising
Purpose for New StudentsHelps new students smoothly adapt to university study life. Provides guidance on major selection, effective study skills development and phased study plan formulation. Introduces diverse extended learning opportunities including exchange, internship, experiential learning, service-learning and residential education beyond formal curriculum.
Continued Support after First YearOffers ongoing guidance for students to update study plans, connect academic learning with post-graduation development, and set clear career goals or further study plans.
Expected Student OutcomesWith academic advising and active participation, students can:
Adapt to the university learning environment efficiently
Select suitable study strategies, majors, concentrations, minors and courses for personal growth and career development
Develop comprehensive study plans and monitor academic progress throughout university
Formulate preparation plans for future careers or postgraduate studies
Utilize university resources to optimize academic performance
Mandatory Advisor Meeting Requirements
First-year students: At least one meeting with academic advisor per semester
Year 2 and above students: Voluntary meetings as needed for academic progress consultation
Mandatory meetings in any academic year under the following circumstances: Declare/change a major; After advisor replacement; Placed on academic probation; Opt out of Transdisciplinary Second Major (TSM)/Second Major (SM)
Types of Academic Advisors & Core Responsibilities
Faculty/Programme Academic Advising Coordinator: Overall coordination of all advising activities, supervise student academic development
Faculty/Programme Academic Advisors: Assist freshmen adaptation, major selection and general study planning; support transdisciplinary curriculum consultation
Department Academic Advisors: Guide major course selection, professional study planning, career transition for Year 1 (programme admission) and Year 2+ students
TSM/SM Academic Staff: Provide TSM/SM programme details and professional course selection advice
Designated Advisors for Individualized Majors: Assist adaptation, major/course selection and personalized study plan formulation
