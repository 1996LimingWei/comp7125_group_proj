# Student Life
Core Units: Student Induction and Guidance, Co-curricular and Extracurricular Activities for Whole Person Education, The Career Centre (CC), Campus Life & Amenities Unit (CLA), Counselling and Development Centre (CDC), Leadership Qualities Centre (LQC), Student Accommodation Section (ACCM)

## Student Induction and Guidance
### New Student Induction
Held annually in August to help new students understand holistic development and adapt to university life. The University Life Orientation Workshop (Personal Transformation) introduces whole person development concepts and campus resources to assist life planning. Senior peer mentors support new students' adaptation. Customised training and programmes are provided for non-local students to adapt to life in Hong Kong smoothly.

### Guidance
Each new student is assigned mentors for academic and personal development guidance. Students must independently check the University Student Handbook for basic regulations, procedures and programme requirements. Final-year students shall proactively consult departments on academic progress and graduation requirements.

### Support from Unit for Students with Special Educational Needs (SEN)
The University provides equal education opportunities for SEN students. Eligible students first register documented SEN information for professional needs assessment. The unit coordinates with departments to offer tailored accommodation, facilities and learning services. Students can apply for disability grants for academic and personal development support.

## Co-curricular and Extracurricular Activities for Whole Person Education
Complementary activities are designed to help students achieve the University's Graduate Attributes and enrich learning experience alongside academic programmes.

### Creative Arts Activities
- Music: Multiple credit-bearing performing groups (orchestras, choirs, ensembles) open to all students regardless of major.
- Visual Arts: On-campus exhibitions, artist talks, guided tours, international art festival visits and community art activities; general education and professional studio courses for all students.
- Film: Masterclasses, special screenings and guest lectures by renowned industry professionals to enhance creativity and career preparation.

### Sports Programmes
Member of the University Sports Federation of Hong Kong; offers intercollegiate competitions for 18 sports for men's and women's teams, intramural sports events and Health Lifestyle general education courses.

### Student Organisations
Voluntary membership open to all students (some with membership fees). Registered/recognised bodies include: Students' Union (undergraduates), Postgraduate Association, over 30 academic societies, around 40 interest clubs, 4 Hall Councils and the International Association (supports international students' integration).

## The Career Centre (CC)
A student-centric hub providing comprehensive resources for career planning, skill improvement and employment matching.

### Core Services
1. Career Planning and Advising: One-on-one personalised consultation for interest assessment, career exploration and development planning.
2. Career Education Programmes: Career recruitment talks, CV writing workshops and interview skill training.
3. Career & Recruitment Events: Physical/online career talks, industry sharing sessions and annual on-campus Career Fair with employer booths.
4. Networking Events: Themed professional networking to connect students with employers and alumni.
5. Exploration Trips: Winter break trips to GBA, Southeast Asia and overseas cities for global workplace experience.
6. Skills Empowerment Scheme: Workshops on business etiquette, AI/GPT, Python, Power BI and ESG; online e-learning resources.
7. Entrepreneurial Support: Training, seed funding and incubation services for startup projects.
8. SEN Exclusive Career Support: Personalised counselling, matched internships and startup competition platforms.

### Internship Opportunities
Local/overseas internships, Metropolitan Attachment Programme (MAP), Global Attachment Opportunities (GAO), GBA targeted internships and STEM specialised internship schemes.

### Job Search Platform: BUhub
AI-enabled portal for internships, graduate jobs and on-campus roles; supports AI CV review, advanced job filtering and personalised matching.

### Postgraduate Exclusive Services
Self-exploration workshops, industry expert consultations and tailored career training for postgraduates. Annual Graduate Employment Survey reports are available for reference.

## Campus Life & Amenities Unit (CLA)
Enriches campus life via facility management, funding support, student governance participation and activity assistance.
1. Support for student bodies: Administrative guidance, training, financial funding for registered student organisations.
2. Amenities management: Operates Learning Commons and various collaborative campus facilities.
3. University Governance Participation: Administers student representative elections for Senate and university committees; organises student consultations with management.

## Counselling and Development Centre (CDC)
Professional psychological team supports students' mental health, personal growth and potential development.

### Core Services
1. Counselling & Assessment: Individual psychotherapy, psychological tests, Whole Person Development Inventory (WPDI) and online self-assessment tests with instant feedback.
2. Psychological Development: Self-growth workshops, Mental Health First Aid courses and online e-learning modules.
3. New Student Support: Transition guidance webpage, Peer Mentor Programme, orientation workshops and whole person development mentoring; Host Family Programme for non-local students.
4. Mainland Student Services: Induction activities, Cantonese courses, individual mentoring and cultural enrichment events for adaptation to Hong Kong life.

## Leadership Qualities Centre (LQC)
Cultivates global citizenship, leadership and community service awareness via diversified non-formal learning.

### Core Programmes
1. Co-curricular Learning (CCL): Seminars, workshops and experiential activities as part of U-Life Programme.
2. Adventure Education: Outdoor challenge activities to build resilience, teamwork and courage.
3. Cultural Literacy (LIVE): Art experience platforms and ambassador training for aesthetic cultivation.
4. Leadership Development: Tailored training; House of Scholars for outstanding academic students; youth leadership mentoring schemes.
5. Global Vision: International conference nominations, Backpacker Learning and overseas Serve-cation volunteer trips.
6. Community Engagement: Skilled volunteer training, long-term NGO internships and social issue funding grants.
7. Student Partnership: Co-develop activities with students; cooperate with external organisations for peer leader training.

## Student Accommodation Section (ACCM)
Manages three types of on-campus residential facilities integrating living, learning and communication.

### Village CARE (Jockey Club Campus of Creativity)
4 residential blocks with 1,726 places; mixed RPG, local/non-local undergraduates and exchange students; 4 themed living-learning colleges with credit-bearing courses, High Table Dinners and community services; equipped with single/2-person rooms, apartments, multi-purpose halls and rooftop activity terraces.

### Undergraduate Halls
4 halls named after four prominent Chinese figures with 1,770 residential places; air-conditioned rooms with LAN/Wi-Fi; public facilities include canteen, fitness rooms, study rooms and kitchens; residential education programmes guided by Resident Masters for all-round development.

### Dr Ng Tor Tai International House
Accommodates visiting scholars and full-time non-local undergraduate/postgraduate students; air-conditioned rooms with private bathrooms and basic appliances; provides regular cleaning, security patrols, shared laundry and dining areas for daily communication.
